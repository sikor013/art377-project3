ABOUT

Mike Abbink joined IBM in August 2020 and an Executive Creative Director. He works closely with design leadership for IBM digital experiences. His most renowned contribution to IBM is designing IBM Plex: the IBM Design Language.

Abbink started his career in 1996 at MetaDesign, and he has worked with many more clients including: Adobe, GE, Mozilla, Nokia, and Visa. His work has earned him many international awards for graphic and typeface design:

  • Pencil Award from D&AD Awards, 2020 for IBM Plex Arabic
  • TDC 64 2018 Judges Choice award for IBM Plex typeface family
  • BFA in Graphic Design and Packaging from the Art Center College of Design

IBM released Plex Sans in September 20, 2023 with the intent to create a font that represents their identity and history. They wish to communicate the unique relationship between man and machine. Plex Sans also has support for multiple scripts, supporting 100 different languages.

IBM Plex is a part of the Sans-serif family. Sans-serif is a font-family where the font lacks serifs: which are essentially tails finishing off a stroke of a letter. Sans-serif fonts are typically used for places where space is a concern, such as apps, map labels, and signs. However, some sans-serif fonts like IBM Plex and Arial can work for body copies or main content text.
